\subsection{July 3 - Feast of St. Thomas, Apostle}

\begin{multicols}{2}
   \paragraph{Entrance Antiphon}~\emph{Cf. Ps 118 (117): 28, 21}
   \small
   You are my God, and I confess you;
   you are my God, and I exalt you;
   I will thank you, for you became my savior.

   \columnbreak

   \paragraph{Introit}~\emph{Cf. Ps 139 (138): 17}
   To me, Your friends, O God, are made exceedingly honorable;
   their principality is exceedingly strengthened.
   
\end{multicols}

\begin{multicols}{2}
   \small
   \paragraph{Resp. Psalm}~
   \emph{Ps 117:1bc, 2}\\
   \textbf{(Mk 16:15) Go out to all the world and tell the Good News.}\\
   Praise the LORD, all you nations;\\
   glorify him, all you peoples! \Rbar

   For steadfast is his kindness for us,\\
   and the fidelity of the LORD endures forever.

   \columnbreak

   \paragraph{Gradual}~
   \textbf{Nimis honorati sunt}\\
   \emph{Ps. 139 (138): 17-18} Your friends, O God, are made
   exceedingly honorable: their principality is exceedingly strengthened.
   \Vbar Were I to recount them, they would outnumber the sands.
   
\end{multicols}


\begin{multicols}{2}
   \paragraph{Alleluia}~\emph{John 20:29}
   \small
   You believe in me, Thomas, because you have seen me, says the Lord;
   blessed are those who have not seen, but still believe!

   \columnbreak

   \paragraph{\emph{Graduale}}~\emph{Ps. 33 (32): 1} Exult, you just, in the Lord;
   praise from the upright is fitting. \\
   \emph{Revised Grail:}
   Ring out your joy to the Lord, O you just;  for praise is fitting from the upright.  

      
\end{multicols}

\paragraph{Offertory Chant} \emph{Ps 19 (18):5}
Through all the earth their voice resounds, and to the ends of the world, their message.\\
\emph{Revised Grail:} Their sound goes forth through all the earth,  their message to the utmost bounds of the world.  


\paragraph{Communion Antiphon/Chant} \emph{Jn. 20:27}
Bring your hand and feel the place of the nails,
and do not be unbelieving but believing.
