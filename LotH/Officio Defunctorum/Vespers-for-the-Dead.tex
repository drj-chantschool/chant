\emph{Before Vespers we will chant the Dies Irae
for the souls in Purgatory and to celebrate the close
of the 2025 liturgical year. Formerly the Sequence for the Requiem

\else
    \emph{ Vespers will begin following the
    solemn chanting of the Dies Irae, in honor of the souls
    of the faithful departed and to celebrate the impending
    close of the 2025 liturgical year.}

    \emph{The Dies Irae was formerly the Sequence for the Requiem
    Mass, to be sung after the Tract (which replaced the Alleluia)
    and before the Gospel. Its somber lyrics remind us of the 
    particular judgment all must face at the end of their lives,
    yet expresses hope in the mercy won for us by Christ's act of
    self sacrifice.}

    \emph{While no longer a mandatory element of the Mass of the Dead,
    the Dies Irae still retains an official place in the Church's 
    liturgy as the hymn for Matins, Lauds and Vespers for the
    last week of Ordinary Time.}
\fi\fi

\iftextonly\else\emph{Recto tono.}\fi
\begin{multicols}{2}
    \iftextonly\Large\else\small\fi
We acknowledge Christ the Lord through whom we hope that our lowly bodies will be made like his in glory, and we say:
\textbf{Lord, you are our life and resurrection.} %\fi

Christ, Son of the living God, who raised up Lazarus, your friend, from the dead,\\
– raise up to life and glory the dead whom you have redeemed by your precious blood.\\
\textbf{Lord, you are our life and resurrection.}

Christ, consoler of those who mourn, you dried the tears of the family of Lazarus, of the widow’s son, and the daughter of Jairus,\\
– comfort those who mourn for the dead.\\
\textbf{Lord, you are our life and resurrection.}

Christ, Savior, destroy the reign of sin in our earthly bodies, so that just as through sin we deserved punishment,\\
– so through you we may gain eternal life.\\
\textbf{Lord, you are our life and resurrection.}

Christ, Redeemer, look on those who have no hope because they do not know you,\\
– may they receive faith in the resurrection and in the life of the world to come.\\
\textbf{Lord, you are our life and resurrection.}

You revealed yourself to the blind man who begged for the light of his eyes,\\
– show your face to the dead who are still deprived of your light.\\
\textbf{Lord, you are our life and resurrection.}

When at last our earthly home is dissolved,\\
– give us a home, not of earthly making, but built of eternity in heaven.\\
\textbf{Lord, you are our life and resurrection.}
\end{multicols}
